Welcome to the Lab

Here we are exploring the world of robotics, both in science fiction and reality, from A - Z.

Artificial Intelligence

The theory and development of computers to perform tasks that would usually require human intelligence to complete.

Bionics

The study of different mechanical and robotic systems that function like or work along with biological systems. Biomedical engineering is mainly concerned with bionics.

Cybernetics

The study and science of automatic control systems within robots. Examples are neural networks that mimic human nervous systems.

Droids

This is a term used in fiction to mean robots. They are usually autonomous machines in science fiction.

Engineering

The science of designing and creating machines and structures. Robotics engineers study designing and machining robotic systems.

Flexibility

A quality of a material to be able to bend without breaking under the stress. In robotics, flexible machines are more adaptive to their environments.
